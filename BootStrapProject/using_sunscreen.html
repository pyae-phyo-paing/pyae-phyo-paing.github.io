How To Use Sunscreen?

Using Sunscreen

Applying sunscreen properly is key to protecting your skin from harmful UV rays. Here’s how to use sunscreen effectively:
- Look for broad-spectrum protection (blocks both UVA and UVB rays).
- SPF 30 or higher is recommended for everyday use.
- Choose a water-resistant formula if swimming or sweating.
- Apply sunscreen 15-30 minutes before going outside to allow it to absorb into the skin.
- For your face, use about a nickel-sized amount.
- For the body, about one ounce (roughly a shot glass full) is needed to cover exposed areas.
- Make sure to cover often-forgotten areas like the ears, neck, tops of feet, and the back of your hands.
- Reapply sunscreen every two hours or immediately after swimming, sweating, or towel drying.
- Apply sunscreen after your moisturizer and before any makeup.
- Don’t forget to protect your lips by using a lip balm with SPF.
- Sunscreen is necessary even on overcast days, as up to 80% of UV rays can penetrate clouds.
By following these steps, you ensure that your skin is properly shielded from harmful UV rays, helping to prevent sunburn, premature aging, and skin cancer.
